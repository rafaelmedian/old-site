My name is Rafael Medina

Rafael Medina is a software developer student who lives in Santo Domingo, Dominican Republic. He is deeply passionate about everything related to computers, best practices and software design. His focus on user interfaces, usability, design and typography allows him to take into account the human side of software development in addition to its purely technical aspects. He’s a fast learner and can switch between technology stacks in the blink of an eye.

Always up-to-date with latest trends in the industry, he’s able to quickly grasp new concepts, methodologies, programming languages and tools.

He has been talking to computers since the age of three and then switched to Linux as his main computer Operating System at the age of twelve and never looked back. He has been a strong advocate for free (as in freedom) and Open Source software ever since.

He has a diverse variety of side interests and likes to express his creativity through writing, digital drawing with Wacom, making websites, producing digital graphics, cooking and learning new things.
